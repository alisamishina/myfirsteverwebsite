Olga Tokarczuk

Olga Tokarczuk (born 1962 in Sulechów, Poland) studied clinical psychology at the University of Warsaw, graduating in 1985, and subsequently worked as a psychotherapist before turning full-time to literature. She has lived between Krajanów and Wrocław since 1998, a borderland region that has directly influenced her writing. Her debut novel, The Journey of the Book-People (1993), won the Polish Publisher's Prize for best debut. Her third novel, Primeval and Other Times (1996), set in a fictitious Polish village chronicled across eight decades through the perspective of four archangels, established her as a leading figure in contemporary Polish literature. House of Day, House of Night (1998), a "constellation novel" composed of loosely connected stories about life in her adopted region of Lower Silesia, was her first book published in English. Flights (2007), a patchwork of fiction and essay exploring modern nomadism and the psychology of travel, won both the jury and audience Nike Awards in 2008 and the Man Booker International Prize in 2018. Drive Your Plow Over the Bones of the Dead (2009), a noir thriller set in the Kłodzko Valley whose title is drawn from William Blake, was a bestseller in Poland and served as the basis for Agnieszka Holland's 2017 film Spoor. The Books of Jacob (2014), widely regarded as her magnum opus, is an epic spanning seven borders, five languages, and three religions, centered on the 18th-century Jewish mystic Jacob Frank; it won the Nike Award in 2015 and the Jan Michalski Prize in 2018. Her most recent novel, The Empusium (2022), inspired by Thomas Mann's The Magic Mountain, addresses misogyny and won the European Literature Prize in 2024. She cites Carl Jung as a primary intellectual influence. In 2019, Tokarczuk was awarded the 2018 Nobel Prize in Literature for "a narrative imagination that with encyclopedic passion represents the crossing of boundaries as a form of life." She has been a vocal critic of antisemitism and Polish nationalism, positions that have generated significant controversy in Poland. In September 2025 she was appointed Vice President of PEN International.
